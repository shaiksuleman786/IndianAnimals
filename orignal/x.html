• Bengal Tiger

    The Bengal tiger, India's national animal, is a powerful and agile predator found in forests and grasslands. Conservation efforts help protect its declining population.

  • Polar Bear

    The polar bear is a large Arctic predator adapted to icy environments, relying on sea ice to hunt seals. Climate change threatens its survival.

  • Red Panda

    The red panda is a small, tree-dwelling mammal native to the Himalayas, known for its reddish fur and bushy tail. It primarily feeds on bamboo and is endangered due to habitat loss.

  • Cheetah

    The cheetah is the fastest land animal, capable of reaching speeds up to 120 km/h. Found in grasslands, it relies on speed to hunt but faces threats from habitat loss.

  • Indian Wolf

    The Indian wolf is a medium-sized, slender wolf found in grasslands and scrub forests of India. It is a skilled hunter but faces threats from habitat loss and human conflict.
